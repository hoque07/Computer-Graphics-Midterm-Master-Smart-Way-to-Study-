1) Introduction to Computer Graphics

Definition & Uses

Computer Graphics is using computers to create, manipulate and display pictures on screens or printers. Typical uses: CAD/CAM, GUI, education & training simulators, visualization, image processing, games, movies, VFX, advertising.

2) Video Display Devices

CRT basics

  • Electron gun ‚Üí focusing & deflection ‚Üí phosphor screen.
  • Raster‚Äëscan: scans rows top‚Üíbottom, 60‚Äì80 Hz using frame buffer.
  • Random‚Äëscan: draws line segments directly (vector display).

Color CRT

  • Beam Penetration (random‚Äëscan): limited colors (red, green, orange, yellow).
  • Shadow Mask (raster‚Äëscan): 3 electron guns (RGB), wide color & realism.

Emissive: Plasma, LED, TFEL. Non‚Äëemissive: LCD.

  • LED: Low power, long life.
  • LCD: Polarizers + liquid crystal + backlight; low power but limited viewing angle, fixed native resolution.

3) DDA Line Algorithm

Idea, Equations & Pseudocode

Step along the driving axis and increment the other by slope \(m=\Delta y/\Delta x\). Plot rounded coordinates.

  • If \(|m|\le 1\): \(x_{k+1}=x_k+1,\; y_{k+1}=y_k+m\)
  • Else: \(y_{k+1}=y_k+1,\; x_{k+1}=x_k+1/m\)
dx=x2-x1; dy=y2-y1
steps=max(|dx|,|dy|)
x_inc=dx/steps; y_inc=dy/steps
x=x1; y=y1
for i=0..steps: plot(round(x),round(y)); x+=x_inc; y+=y_inc
Floating‚Äëpoint rounding Slower than Bresenham

Solved Example (1 ‚Üí 4, slope 2/3)

From (1,1) to (4,3): points ‚âà (1,1), (2,1.67), (3,2.34), (4,3.01) ‚Üí rounded: (1,1),(2,2),(3,2),(4,3).

4) Bresenham Line Algorithm

Derivation & Update

Choose between E=(x+1,y) and NE=(x+1,y+1) by a decision variable. Integer‚Äëonly arithmetic.

  • Init: \(p_0=2\Delta y-\Delta x\)
  • If \(p_k<0\): E ‚Üí \(p_{k+1}=p_k+2\Delta y\)
  • Else: NE ‚Üí \(p_{k+1}=p_k+2\Delta y-2\Delta x\)

Solved Example (9,18)‚Üí(14,22)

dx=5, dy=4, p0=3 ‚Üí sequence: (9,18),(10,19),(11,19),(12,20),(13,21),(14,22).

Swap x/y for steep slopes Reflect for negative slopes

5) Midpoint Circle Algorithm

Key Ideas & Recurrence

Use circle function \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2-r^2\) and the midpoint between E and SE to decide the next pixel in the first octant; mirror to 8 octants.

  • Init at (0,r): \(p_0=1-r\)
  • If \(p_k<0\): choose E ‚Üí \(p_{k+1}=p_k+2x_{k+1}+1\)
  • Else: choose SE ‚Üí \(p_{k+1}=p_k+2x_{k+1}+1-2y_{k+1}\)

Solved Example (r=10)

Start (0,10), p0=‚àí9 ‚Üí (1,10),(2,10),(3,9),(4,9),(5,8),(6,7),(7,7) stop when x>y; reflect to all octants.

10) Previous Years (Questions & Model Answers)

Q1. Differentiate Raster‚Äëscan and Random‚Äëscan displays.

Ans: Raster: scans line‚Äëby‚Äëline using frame buffer; supports full color, good for images; may flicker. Random: draws specified line segments directly; high quality lines, limited color; suits vector graphics.

Q2. Explain DVST working with a neat sketch.

Ans: Uses a primary writing gun to charge storage mesh forming the image; a flood gun maintains visibility without refresh; high‚Äëpersistence phosphor ‚Üí flicker‚Äëfree; cannot do partial erase; best for static images.

Q3. Using DDA, draw a line from (1,1) to (4,3).

Ans: dx=3, dy=2 ‚Üí steps=3 ‚Üí x_inc=1, y_inc=0.67. Rounded points: (1,1), (2,2), (3,2), (4,3).

Q4. Using Bresenham, draw a line from (2,2) to (9,6).

Ans: dx=7, dy=4 ‚Üí p0=1. Plot: (2,2) NE‚Üí(3,3) E‚Üí(4,3) NE‚Üí(5,4) E‚Üí(6,4) NE‚Üí(7,5) E‚Üí(8,5) NE‚Üí(9,6).

Q5. Midpoint circle: r=8, center (0,0). Generate octant points.

Ans: p0=1‚àí8=‚àí7. Sequence (x,y): (0,8) E‚Üí(1,8) E‚Üí(2,8) E‚Üí(3,7) SE‚Üí(4,7) E‚Üí(5,6) SE‚Üí(6,6) SE‚Üí(7,5). Reflect to all octants.

Q6. Apply rotation by 30¬∞ then scaling (1.5, 1.5) about origin to point (2,0).

Ans: Rotate: (2cos30¬∞, 2sin30¬∞)=(1.732,1). Scale ‚Üí (2.598,1.5).

Q7. What is anti‚Äëaliasing? Mention two techniques.

Ans: Reducing jagged edges due to sampling; techniques: area/prefiltering and supersampling + post‚Äëfiltering.

Q8. Compare LED vs LCD.

Ans: LED emits light directly (via diodes), high brightness & efficiency; LCD modulates backlight using liquid crystals, low power but limited viewing angle and requires backlight.
